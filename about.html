Our Story

Agriculture Meets Artificial Intelligence

AgriMitra was born from a simple conviction: every Indian farmer deserves access to the same quality of agronomic expertise available to large-scale industrial farms — regardless of their land size or location.

Mission

Why We Built AgriMitra

India is home to over 140 million farming households. Yet most of these farmers make critical decisions — which crop to plant, when to irrigate, how to identify disease — based on experience alone, without access to real-time data or expert guidance.

AgriMitra changes that. By combining soil science, satellite climate data, and state-of-the-art machine learning models, we deliver personalised, actionable insights directly to farmers — in their language, on their devices.

Our crop recommendation engine analyses 9 soil and climate parameters to suggest the most profitable and sustainable crops for any field. Our disease detection model, trained on over 50,000 annotated leaf images, identifies 22 diseases across 14 crop types with 94%+ accuracy.

What Drives Us

Our Core Values

🌱

Farmer-First Design

Every feature is built around real farmer workflows. We conduct regular field visits and user research to ensure the tool works in the ground, not just in the lab.

🔬

Science-Backed Insights

Our recommendations are grounded in peer-reviewed agronomy research and validated ML models — not guesswork. We publish our model cards and accuracy benchmarks openly.

🌍

Sustainability

We prioritise crop rotation, soil health, and water efficiency in every recommendation — because a good harvest today must not come at the cost of tomorrow's soil.

🔒

Data Privacy

Farmer data is never sold or shared with third parties. Soil and location data is anonymised and stored securely with end-to-end encryption.

🤝

Inclusivity

AgriMitra supports multiple regional languages and works on low-bandwidth connections — because access to good information should not depend on a fast internet plan.

📖

Transparency

We explain why we recommend what we recommend. Every suggestion comes with interpretable reasoning so farmers can make informed choices, not just take our word for it.
